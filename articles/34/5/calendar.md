### Thursday, Dec. 4

Eastford Public Library will host "Story Time" at 11 a.m. Join us for a
story, craft, snack and time of free play.  

The Congregational Church of Eastford announces our annual free Senior
Community Christmas Dinner on Thursday, Dec. 4! Open to seniors aged 55+
(caregivers welcome!). RSVP required before Nov. 28. [Online signup at the church website here](https://cceastford.org/christmas-dinner/), or call (860) 974-0294 with
the names of people in your party. The dinner will be held at the

### Saturday, Dec. 6

Mark your calendar for some free, family fun! On Saturday, Dec. 6, the
Recreation Commission will host its annual Cookies & Cocoa with Santa
event from 2-4 p.m. at Eastford Elementary School (snow date Dec. 13).
Crafts, treats, cocoa and more!

The Friends of the Eastford Public Library will be hosting its 2nd
Annual Holiday Fair on Saturday, Dec. 6, from 9 a.m. to 1 p.m. Please
join us for a silent auction, hand-crafted gifts, baked goods and so
much more. All proceeds and financial contributions from the Holiday
Fair will be used to directly support our library. Help us have another
successful year! 

### Sunday, Dec. 7

December is here already, after another warm fall. Join us for our
traditional "Lunch with Santa" fundraiser at the Windham-Tolland 4-H
Camp on Sunday, Dec. 7, from 11 a.m. to 1 p.m., to get into the holiday
season early. Have lunch upstairs in the lodge or just come for a photo
with Santa and avoid the lines at the mall. Made to order Pasta Bar,
salad, drinks and desserts (gluten free pasta available). Join us in
making holiday crafts, cookies, S'mores and more, and shop our selection
of silent auction and raffle items. Tickets available ahead of time by
calling (860) 974-3379, e-mail <registrar@4hcampct.org>, or at the door.
No reservations necessary. Lunch tickets are $14 for those 10 and
older, $10 for children age 9 and under. A 5x7 photo with Santa will be
$8. Funds raised support the Jim Logee Campership Fund.

Day Kimball Hospital's Hospice Tree of Life Ceremony will be held
Sunday, Dec. 7, at 5 p.m. Join us in front of the Ivy Glenn
Memorial/Eastford Public Library for a time of remembrance and tree
lighting followed by refreshments. Each December, communities throughout
Northeast Connecticut come together to remember and honor their loved
ones through a symbol of light. The purchase of memorial lights on each
community's Tree of Life benefits the wonderful work done by Hospice &
Palliative Care of Northeastern Connecticut to enhance the lives of
terminally ill patients and their families. [For more information, visit the Day Kimball website here](https://www.daykimball.org/hospice-tree-of-life/).

### Sunday, Dec. 14

The Congregational Church of Eastford is offering a 13-week support
group for people undergoing separation or divorce. The sessions will
begin Sunday, Dec. 14, running 13 consecutive Sundays from 5:30-7 p.m.
All sessions will be held at the church office, 4 County Rd., Eastford.
Some of the topics covered during this helpful course include
acknowledging deep hurt, dealing with children and co-parenting issues,
financial and legal issues, forgiveness and much more. For questions,
please reach out to the facilitator at: <cceastford@gmail.com>. It is
requested that you [register at the church website](https://cceastford.org/divorcecare/).
