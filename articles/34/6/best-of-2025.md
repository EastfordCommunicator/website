## January

The New Year began with a freeze. Scouts remained busy in January with
their annual Rain Gutter Regatta event. Meanwhile, the Eastford Bulldogs
basketball team concluded their tough season on Jan. 27 without a win.

## February

Community events picked up steam in February, as Scramble the Duck made
his long-awaited return to predict an early spring on Feb. 2; Linnea
Barlow and Abigail Budd enjoyed a sweet treat at the Congregational
Church of Eastford's 7th Annual Cupcake Wars on Feb. 15; and Kara
Norman claimed her third straight Olympians Lego Derby title at Eastford
Baptist Church on Feb. 22. *File photos*

## March

Eastford Elementary School students celebrated Read Across America on
March 4 by reading stories to Reading Education Assistant Dogs (READ).
Meanwhile, Grove Cemetery Association hosted a spring clean-up at the
cemetery on Wednesday, March 19. Over at Eastford Baptist Church, Chili
Cookoff winners Sally Rempel, Dave Hustus, and Herm Barlow were crowned
on March 23. *File photos*

## April

In April, the Pomfret/Eastford Little League (PELL) hosted a cleanup at
Bowen Memorial Field on April 19; Eastford Baptist Church and the
Congregational Church of Eastford joined forces on April 6 to conduct a
Roadside Cleanup Day; and the Recreation Commission hosted its annual
Easter Egg Hunt on April 19, providing a fun morning of egg hunting fun
for kids. *File photos*

## May

In May, Sloane Volpe was one of many kids who enjoyed the Experience
Eastford event on May 10. Meanwhile, Eastford held its annual Memorial

## June

In June, Eastford Elementary School sent off the Class of 2025 at its
annual Graduation Ceremony on June 12. Earlier in the month, the class
visited Washington D.C. for the school's annual field trip, including a
visit to Arlington National Cemetery and the gravesite of Joseph E.
Phaneuf II, a Staff Sergeant in the U.S. Army from Eastford who was
killed in Afghanistan in 2006. Earlier in the month, the Congregational
Church of Eastford hosted a groundbreaking ceremony on June 1 for its
new church location. *File photos*

## July

In July, the Congregational Church of Eastford hosted its annual Frog
Jump and Band Concert at Crystal Pond Park on July 12, which included
the fan-favorite "Cake Walk." Meanwhile, the Congregational Church of
Eastford and Living Proof Church of Ashford held its annual Sports Camp
at Eastford Elementary School July 21-25. Down the road, Eastford
Baptist Church held its annual Vacation Bible School July 28-Aug. 1,
which included an appearance by the dastardly "Grump." *File photos*

## September

In September, the Pomfret Eastford Little League (PELL) celebrated
opening night of their annual Fall Ball season on Sept. 8. Meanwhile, on
Sept. 13, the Connecticut Sons of the American Revolution hosted a
Liberty Tree dedication in honor of the United States Constitution and
the 250th anniversary of the United States. At right, Callum St. John
races after the ball during the Bulldogs' 9-2 loss against Pomfret on
Tuesday, Sept. 30. *File photos*

## October

In October, Buell's Orchard held a pair of events that brought the
community out to enjoy some family fun. Their annual Trunk or Treat
event was held Oct. 26, featuring more than a dozen trunks for families
to enjoy. Earlier in the month, the orchard held its annual Harvest
Festival on Oct. 11. Meanwhile, the town hosted its annual Trunk or
Treat on Halloween night, Oct. 31, at Eastford Elementary School, which
brought out children and their families to enjoy a myriad of treats.

## November

In November, Eastford veterans, along with several other surrounding
towns, were honored in a Veterans Day ceremony at E.O. Smith High School
in Mansfield on Nov. 8. Meanwhile, on Nov. 4, Eastford held its
Municipal Election, and several were sworn in during a brief ceremony on
Nov. 17. Over at Eastford Elementary School, Georgia Barlow was selected
as the winner of the PTO's annual sign contest on Nov. 12. *File
photos*

## December

In December, the Eastford Bulldogs won their first Varsity basketball
game in nearly three years with a 43-33 victory over Parish Hill on Dec. 8. Meanwhile, the Recreation Commission hosted its annual "Cookies &
Cocoa with Santa" event on Dec. 6. On Dec. 7, Day Kimball Healthcare's
36th Annual Hospice Tree of Life Ceremony was held at the Ivy Glenn
Memorial, where Justin Loss lit the tree in honor of his grandmother,
Helene, who died in December 2024. *File photos*
